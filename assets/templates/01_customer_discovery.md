## Problem interview script

1. What is your role and what are you responsible for?
2. Walk me through how you currently handle [workflow/problem].
3. Tell me about the last time this came up.
4. What triggered it?
5. What did you do first? Then what?
6. What tools, people, documents, or vendors were involved?
7. What was frustrating, slow, expensive, risky, or political?
8. What happened because of the problem? Time, money, risk, missed revenue, stress?
9. How often does this happen?
10. Who else cares when this goes wrong?
11. What have you tried before?
12. What do you use today instead?
13. What do you pay today in tools, people, time, consultants, or opportunity cost?
14. What would make this urgent enough to fix this quarter?
15. What would block a solution from being adopted?
16. Who would approve budget?
17. What would need to be true for this to be worth [$X]?
18. Can I show you a rough approach and get your reaction?
19. Would you be willing to [specific commitment: intro, data, pilot, preorder, follow-up]?

## Switch interview script

Use for current customers, churned users, or competitor users.

| Moment | Questions |
|---|---|
| First thought | When did you first realize the old way might not work? |
| Passive looking | What did you notice or read before actively searching? |
| Active looking | What alternatives did you evaluate? |
| Decision | What made you choose? Who was involved? |
| Anxiety | What almost stopped you? |
| Habit/inertia | What made the old way hard to leave? |
| First value | When did you know the new solution was working? |
| Language | What words would you use to describe the problem to a peer? |

## Red flags

- Interviewee says the idea is interesting but will not commit to any next step.
- Pain exists but no current workaround, spend, deadline, or owner.
- Buyer uses vague future language: “I might,” “maybe,” “could see this being useful.”
- The founder speaks more than the buyer.
- The only strong signal comes from friends, advisors, or other founders outside ICP.
