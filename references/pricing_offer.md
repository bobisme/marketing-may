Load when choosing a pricing model, defining a value metric, evaluating WTP evidence, designing a paid offer, or structuring risk reversal.

## Pricing principles

- Price around value and buyer economics, not only cost.
- Use competitor pricing to understand anchors, not to copy.
- Low price is not automatically low friction; it can signal low value or wrong ICP.
- The value metric must be legible, controllable, measurable, and correlated with customer value.
- Packaging should make the right customer self-select and the wrong customer opt out.
- Discounts are not a strategy; use them to learn, compensate for risk, or accelerate annual/prepaid commitment.
- Avoid bill shock. Provide caps, usage visibility, alerts, and plain-language billing examples.

## Pricing model decision table

| Model | Best for | Watchouts | Tests |
|---|---|---|---|
| Flat monthly | Simple tools, indie products | Caps expansion, may underprice power users | Price ladder, annual discount |
| Tiered packages | Most SaaS/products | Feature gating can be arbitrary | Package comprehension test, pricing page A/B |
| Per seat | Collaboration/team tools | Penalizes adoption and shared accounts | Seat expansion data, team activation |
| Usage-based | Infra/API/AI/data where usage maps to value | Bill shock, unpredictable margins | Usage calculator, spend caps, metric interviews |
| Hybrid base + usage | Variable usage with support needs | Complexity | Pilot invoices, onboarding script |
| Outcome-based | Clear attributable value | Attribution disputes, risk | Paid pilot, contract terms |
| Transaction/take-rate | Marketplaces/payments | Margin, fraud, disintermediation | Cohort unit economics |
| Services/concierge | Early validation, complex adoption | Not scalable by default | Concierge MVP, fixed-scope pilot |

## Value metric fit score

```text
Value Metric Fit =
(Value correlation × Predictability × Customer controllability × Measurement clarity × Expansion headroom)
− (Bill shock risk + Margin volatility + Gaming risk)
```

Reject a value metric if customers cannot estimate it before signup or verify it after billing.

## WTP evidence ladder

| Level | Evidence | Strength |
|---:|---|---|
| 1 | Buyer says they would pay | Weak |
| 2 | Buyer names current spend/budget | Moderate |
| 3 | Buyer accepts price in interview without heavy prompting | Moderate |
| 4 | Buyer chooses package in survey or fake pricing page | Stronger |
| 5 | Buyer signs LOI or procurement next step | Strong |
| 6 | Buyer prepays, deposits, or starts paid pilot | Very strong |
| 7 | Buyer renews/expands | Strongest |

Use Van Westendorp or Gabor-Granger for directional ranges. Use paid pilots, preorders, and renewal behavior for truth.

## Pricing research questions

Ask about past/current spend first:

- "How do you solve this today?"
- "What tools, people, or time does that require?"
- "What does it cost when this goes wrong?"
- "Who owns the budget?"
- "What would make this a no-brainer?"
- "What price would feel suspiciously cheap?"
- "What price would feel expensive but still worth considering?"
- "At what point would it be too expensive to consider?"
- "What would you need to see to approve this?"

Avoid:

- "Would you buy this?"
- "Do you like this idea?"
- "What should we build?"
- "Would $X be okay?" before understanding value.

## Offer design

An offer is not just product + price. It is the buyer's path from uncertainty to value.

### Offer components

| Component | Question | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Promise | What outcome? | "Find broken revenue tracking in 48 hours." |
| Buyer | For whom? | "For B2B SaaS founders using Stripe + GA4." |
| Mechanism | Why this works? | "Event audit + revenue-path replay." |
| Package | What is included? | Audit, report, fixes, dashboard, follow-up |
| Price | What commitment? | $500 fixed fee, credited to annual plan |
| Proof | Why trust it? | Sample report, references, benchmark |
| Risk reversal | Why safe? | Refund if no tracking issues found |
| CTA | What next? | Book audit, upload access, start trial |

### Risk reversal menu

Use carefully; do not promise what cannot be delivered.

| Risk | Reversal |
|---|---|
| Product doesn't work | Free trial, sandbox, sample output, paid pilot |
| Buyer wastes time | Concierge setup, done-with-you onboarding, templates |
| Buyer pays and regrets | Refund window, credit toward annual plan, cancellation clarity |
| Internal stakeholder blocks | Decision memo, ROI calculator, security pack |
| Migration is painful | Migration checklist, import service, coexistence mode |
| Vendor disappears | Roadmap, support SLA, founder access, escrow for enterprise |
