# Customer Discovery

Load when designing or running customer interviews, problem discovery, or switch interviews.

## Contents

- Interview rules
- Interview structure
- Commitment menu

## Interview rules

- Ask about real past behavior, not imagined future behavior.
- Talk about their life and workflow before mentioning the product.
- Seek specifics: last time, tools used, time spent, money spent, who was involved, what broke.
- Listen for emotion, urgency, workaround, budget, and switching attempts.
- Ignore compliments unless paired with commitment.
- End with a concrete ask: intro, pilot, deposit, data, next meeting, procurement step.

## Interview structure

| Stage | Question |
|---|---|
| Context | "Tell me about your role and how [workflow] works today." |
| Last occurrence | "Walk me through the last time this happened." |
| Cost | "What happened because of it? Time, money, risk, people?" |
| Current alternative | "How do you solve it today?" |
| Trigger | "What made you look for a better way?" |
| Buying process | "Who cares? Who approves? What budget does it come from?" |
| Alternatives | "What else did you try or consider?" |
| Objections | "What would make you hesitate to switch?" |
| WTP | "What does the current solution cost?" then "What would need to be true for this to be worth $X?" |
| Commitment | "Would you be willing to [specific next step]?" |

## Commitment menu

| Commitment | Signal strength |
|---|---:|
| Intro to colleague | Medium |
| Share workflow/data | Medium |
| Second meeting with stakeholder | Strong |
| Join design partner cohort | Strong |
| Sign LOI | Very strong |
| Paid pilot/deposit | Strongest before full product |
