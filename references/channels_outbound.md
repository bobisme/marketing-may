# Sales Motion, Channels & Cold Outbound

Load when choosing a sales motion, comparing channels, planning outbound, or evaluating ACV/channel fit.

## Contents

- Sales motion decision tree
- ACV/channel fit heuristic
- Channel selection algorithm
- Cold outbound system (fit checklist, template, decision thresholds)

## Sales motion decision tree

Choose the sales motion before channel strategy.

| Condition | Recommended motion |
|---|---|
| Low price, simple value, fast setup | Self-serve trial or freemium |
| Moderate price, some complexity | Product-led sales, demo-assisted trial, onboarding call |
| High ACV, committee, risk, procurement | Sales-led with proof, security, ROI, pilot |
| Unclear product, high service need | Concierge MVP / paid diagnostic |
| Marketplace/network effects | Supply/demand wedge, community, partnerships |
| Developer/API product | Docs-led, examples, free tier, usage-based, community support |

## ACV/channel fit heuristic

| ACV/AOV | Likely viable channels |
|---:|---|
| <$50 | Organic, product virality, marketplaces, community, low-cost content |
| $50–$500 | SEO/AEO, paid with tight CAC, affiliates, lifecycle email |
| $500–$5k | Founder-led content, outbound, webinars, demos, partnerships |
| $5k–$50k | Targeted outbound, ABM-lite, pilots, events, partner referrals |
| $50k+ | Enterprise sales, procurement assets, executive narrative, references |

## Channel selection algorithm

```text
Channel Fit =
Intent Density × ICP Reachability × Message Velocity × Proof Fit × CAC Payback Feasibility × Learning Speed
− Platform Risk − Operational Burden − Trust/Compliance Risk
```

| Channel | Works when | Fails when | Low-budget first test |
|---|---|---|---|
| Founder-led content | Buyer trusts expertise; category needs education | No clear POV, no consistency | 10 posts from interview insights |
| SEO/AEO | Existing search intent | Category has no search language | 5 use-case pages, track qualified CTA |
| Paid search | High-intent keywords, high enough ACV | Low conversion or crowded auctions | Exact-match campaign to offer page |
| Paid social | Visual/social proof, retargeting, broad demand | B2B niche without proof | Retarget site visitors or test pain angles |
| Cold outbound | Named buyer, clear trigger, high value | Generic list, weak offer | 50 personalized trigger-based emails |
| Partnerships | Shared ICP, trust transfer | No mutual value | 5 integration/co-marketing pitches |
| Community | Buyers congregate and discuss pain | Pure promotion unwelcome | Answer 20 pain threads, collect language |
| Marketplace/app store | Buyers shop there | Weak reviews/onboarding | Listing + install activation audit |
| Webinars/events | Complex sale, education needed | No audience or proof | Partner webinar with diagnostic offer |
| Referrals | Happy users and networked buyers | Low retention or unclear ask | Referral prompt after activation |

## Cold outbound system

Outbound works when message, list, trigger, and offer are specific.

### Outbound fit checklist

- Named role can feel the pain.
- Trigger is observable: hiring, funding, migration, launch, regulation, outage, job post, review complaint, tool adoption.
- The message references a specific business context.
- Offer is a useful next step, not "see my product."
- CTA is low-friction and appropriate to ACV.
- Email complies with applicable laws and includes required identity/opt-out elements.

### Cold email template

```text
Subject: [specific trigger/problem] at [company]

Hi [Name] — noticed [specific trigger].

Teams in [context] often run into [pain/cost] when [workflow/event]. [Product/offer] helps [role] [outcome] by [mechanism], without [common objection].

Concrete example: [proof, benchmark, sample, or relevant observation].

Worth a quick look if I send over [asset/audit/example], or should I ask someone else on your team?

[Name]
[Company]
[Physical mailing address if commercial email compliance requires]
[Opt-out line]
```

### Outbound decision thresholds

For 50–100 highly targeted emails:

| Result | Interpretation | Action |
|---|---|---|
| <1% positive replies | Bad list, weak trigger, weak offer, or wrong segment | Rewrite or narrow before scaling |
| 1–5% positive replies | Some signal | Segment replies, test 2nd angle |
| 5–10% positive replies | Strong for cold B2B | Book calls, test pricing/offer |
| >10% positive replies | Very strong | Build repeatable motion, add proof |

Positive replies mean relevant interest, not "thanks" or auto-responses.
