# Bibliography and Research Anchors

This skill distills practical patterns from product marketing, analytics, experimentation, pricing research, UX, behavioral science, and regulatory guidance. Retrieve live sources when applying the skill to a specific product, because competitor pricing, platform rules, analytics tools, and laws change.

## Strategy, category, and market entry

- MIT Sloan, “A strategic playbook for entrepreneurs: 4 paths to success” (2025). Useful for the choice-first view of startup strategy: customers, technology, organization, competition; commercialization paths depend on compete/collaborate and execution/control choices. https://mitsloan.mit.edu/ideas-made-to-matter/a-strategic-playbook-entrepreneurs-4-paths-to-success
- Harvard Business Review, “Strategy for Start-ups” (2018). Strategy compass and startup path selection. https://hbr.org/2018/05/strategy-for-start-ups
- Harvard Business Review, “The Difference Between a First Mover and a Category Creator” (2019). Useful for category creation vs first-mover framing. https://hbr.org/2019/11/the-difference-between-a-first-mover-and-a-category-creator

## Jobs-to-be-Done and customer discovery

- Christensen Institute, “Jobs to Be Done Theory.” Defines JTBD as the circumstances and forces driving people and organizations toward and away from decisions; emphasizes functional, emotional, and social dimensions. https://www.christenseninstitute.org/theory/jobs-to-be-done/
- HBR, “Know Your Customers’ Jobs to Be Done” (2016). Classic management article on why customers hire products. https://hbr.org/2016/09/know-your-customers-jobs-to-be-done
- Rob Fitzpatrick, *The Mom Test* (book). Practical customer interview discipline: ask about past behavior and real problems, not whether people like your idea.

## Pricing and packaging

- Y Combinator Startup Library, “How To Price For B2B” (2024). Founder-oriented guidance on B2B pricing and buyer justification. https://www.ycombinator.com/library/LI-how-to-price-for-b2b
- Y Combinator Startup Library, “Startup Pricing 101” (2019). Startup monetization fundamentals. https://www.ycombinator.com/library/6h-startup-pricing-101
- Stripe, “Usage-based pricing strategy for SaaS” (last updated 2026). Value metrics should scale with value, be legible before signup, and be clearly measurable; usage pricing needs metering, rating, invoicing, spend visibility, and migration sequencing. https://stripe.com/resources/more/usage-based-pricing-strategy-for-saas
- Stripe, “A guide to SaaS pricing models” (2023). Overview of flat-rate, tiered, per-seat, usage-based, and hybrid pricing. https://stripe.com/resources/more/saas-pricing-models-101
- SurveyMonkey, “Market research for pricing.” Summary of Van Westendorp, Gabor-Granger, conjoint analysis, and brand-price tradeoff methods. https://www.surveymonkey.com/market-research/resources/market-research-pricing/

## Funnel analytics, product analytics, and instrumentation

- Google Analytics, “Recommended events.” GA4 guidance on optional/recommended events and event parameters. https://developers.google.com/analytics/devguides/collection/ga4/reference/events
- Google Analytics Help, “Key event attribution models report.” Attribution models compare how key events are credited across touchpoints; model choice affects channel valuation. https://support.google.com/analytics/answer/10596865
- Amplitude, “From Chaos to Clarity: How to Scale Your Analytics Taxonomy” (2026). Event taxonomy as the way teams choose, name, and organize events/properties; inconsistent or bloated taxonomies block insight. https://amplitude.com/blog/from-chaos-to-clarity
- Amplitude, “The Amplitude Guide to Product Metrics.” Lifecycle product metrics and measurement framing. https://amplitude.com/product-metrics
- Mixpanel, “What is a metric tree?” (2025). Metric trees bridge strategy and execution by linking focus metrics to input metrics. https://mixpanel.com/blog/metric-tree/

## Experimentation

- Microsoft Research, “Diagnosing Sample Ratio Mismatch in A/B Testing” (2020). SRM checks are necessary before trusting A/B test results; missing users are often the users most affected by a treatment. https://www.microsoft.com/en-us/research/articles/diagnosing-sample-ratio-mismatch-in-a-b-testing/
- Kohavi, Tang, and Xu, *Trustworthy Online Controlled Experiments* (book, 2020). Practical guide to A/B testing, guardrails, interpretation pitfalls, and trustworthy experimentation.
- Optimizely Support, “Understand your Experiment Scorecard” (2026). Differentiates decision-making metrics and guardrail metrics. https://support.optimizely.com/hc/en-us/articles/34053132157965-Understand-your-Experiment-Scorecard
- Harvard Business Review / HBR IdeaCast, “Great Strategy Starts with Experimentation” (2025). Stefan Thomke on experimentation as a strategy discipline. https://hbr.org/podcast/2025/04/great-strategy-starts-with-experimentation

## Conversion UX, copy, and trust

- Nielsen Norman Group, “Ecommerce User Experience Vol. 09: Trust and Credibility.” Trust is essential to users risking time, money, and personal data; report covers factors that enhance/damage credibility. https://www.nngroup.com/reports/ecommerce-ux-trust-and-credibility/
- Baymard Institute, “E-Commerce Cart & Checkout Usability Research.” Checkout UX benchmark and conversion improvement opportunities. https://baymard.com/research/checkout-usability
- CXL, “Use Voice-of-Customer Research to Boost Conversions” (updated 2024). Voice-of-customer research mines customer language, needs, motivations, and gaps to improve copy and tests. https://cxl.com/blog/voice-of-customer/
- Google Search Central, “AI features and your website.” Existing SEO fundamentals remain relevant for AI Overviews/AI Mode; no special schema or AI text file is required. https://developers.google.com/search/docs/appearance/ai-features
- Google Search Central, “SEO Starter Guide.” SEO fundamentals focused on helping search engines understand content and helping users decide to visit. https://developers.google.com/search/docs/fundamentals/seo-starter-guide

## Buyer psychology

- University of Chicago News, “Behavioral economics, explained.” Overview of Tversky, Kahneman, framing, loss aversion, heuristics, and nudges. https://news.uchicago.edu/explainer/what-is-behavioral-economics
- Kahneman and Tversky, “Prospect Theory: An Analysis of Decision under Risk” (Econometrica, 1979). Foundational work on decisions under risk, framing, and loss aversion.
- Samuelson and Zeckhauser, “Status Quo Bias in Decision Making” (Journal of Risk and Uncertainty, 1988). Foundational work showing people disproportionately stick with current/default options.
- American Psychological Association, “The power of persuasion, with Robert Cialdini, PhD.” Overview of persuasion principles including social proof. https://www.apa.org/news/podcasts/speaking-of-psychology/persuasion

## B2B buying and trust

- Gartner, “The B2B Buying Journey.” Gartner reports many B2B buyers prefer rep-free digital experiences but also warns that fully self-service digital purchases can lead to regret; hybrid digital/human support matters. https://www.gartner.com/en/sales/insights/b2b-buying-journey
- Edelman, “2025 Edelman Trust Barometer.” Global trust survey methodology and findings; useful for the importance of trust, transparency, and grievance-sensitive communication. https://www.edelman.com/trust/2025/trust-barometer

## Compliance, ethics, and claims

- FTC, “Advertisement Endorsements.” Endorsers should not make claims without proof; material connections should be disclosed; 2023 Endorsement Guide revisions address social media and reviews. https://www.ftc.gov/news-events/topics/truth-advertising/advertisement-endorsements
- FTC, “CAN-SPAM Act: A Compliance Guide for Business.” Commercial email requirements, identity, subject lines, ad disclosure, physical address, opt-out, and penalties. https://www.ftc.gov/business-guidance/resources/can-spam-act-compliance-guide-business
- FTC, “Policy Statement Regarding Advertising Substantiation.” Firms lacking a reasonable basis before disseminating an ad violate Section 5. https://www.ftc.gov/legal-library/browse/ftc-policy-statement-regarding-advertising-substantiation
- FTC, “FTC Report Shows Rise in Sophisticated Dark Patterns Designed to Trick and Trap Consumers.” Dark-pattern examples include disguised ads, difficult cancellation, buried terms, junk fees, and tricking users into sharing data. https://www.ftc.gov/news-events/news/press-releases/2022/09/ftc-report-shows-rise-sophisticated-dark-patterns-designed-trick-trap-consumers
- FTC, “Health Claims.” Health-related product claims require appropriate substantiation and scientific support. https://www.ftc.gov/business-guidance/advertising-marketing/health-claims
- GDPR legal text and overview. Use for EU/EEA data/privacy constraints; consult counsel for application. https://gdpr-info.eu/
